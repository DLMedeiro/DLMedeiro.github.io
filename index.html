About Me

My name is Danielle Aranda. I graduated from Cal Poly San Luis Obispo in 2013 with a degree in Industrial Engineering. In 2020, I decided to become a software developer.

I chose a career in industrial engineering because of my interest in solving complex problems and improving human factors. Each career move since graduation has allowed me the opportunity to work with amazing people, take part in extremely exciting technologies, and has helped shaped the path I am on today. I started in the manufacturing industry with Extron Electronics, bringing products from conception to production. As a green college graduate, Extron confirmed my ability to learn and adapt. Anxious for a new challenge, my limits were found with Flex, where I reached new extents of my professional capabilities and also learned the importance of a sustainable work ethic.

After a few years in the manufacturing space, I decided to bring my focus back to process improvement through auditing. As a Process Analyst with Amazon, I found my love for learning, specifically how people and processes work together. In this role, I soon realized having a hands-on involvement was important and I wanted to actively participate in solving the problems I was observing. This led to my most recent role as a materials manager, where I was able to manage my own space and run with my ideas to solve the systems I was working with on a daily basis. For the first time in my career, I was part of a team that put a heavy focus on career development and creating opportunities for employees to reach their goals. This exploration led to the realization I was missing the passion and excitement of my peers, and it seemed another parallel move was in my future.

Like a perfect storm, this all came together in mid-2020, and like many, provided an ideal opportunity to step back and become more inquisitive in my past and future career moves. I placed more value on personal well-being and mental growth, and focused on the areas of life that energized me and how I spent my free time. This culminated in my decision to pursue a career in software development with the goal of creating software solutions that have a positive impact on healthcare and/or education systems.
